Master's Thesis

Master's Thesis: Sapienza University of Rome

Self-Supervised Learning for 3D Point Cloud Understanding

In my master's thesis, I focused on advancing the performance of state-of-the-art (SOTA) self-supervised learning (SSL) techniques for 3D point cloud understanding. 3D point clouds, which capture the geometry and spatial arrangement of objects, are pivotal in domains such as robotics, augmented reality, and autonomous driving. However, their irregular structure, inherent noise, and the scarcity of labeled data present significant challenges for traditional supervised learning approaches. My research leveraged SSL to mitigate these limitations by enabling models to learn more effectively from unlabeled 3D data, reducing dependency on costly annotations.

The cornerstone of my work was the optimization of CrossPoint, a leading cross-modal contrastive learning architecture. CrossPoint utilizes both 3D point clouds and their corresponding 2D image representations to improve feature extraction and representation learning. This architecture integrates Dynamic Graph Convolutional Neural Networks (DGCNN) for 3D data and Residual Networks (ResNet) for 2D data, aligning features across modalities to leverage their complementary strengths. Building upon this foundation, I incorporated VICReg (Variance-Invariance-Covariance Regularization), a robust SSL framework that enhances embedding stability by preserving variance, reducing redundancy, and preventing collapse in joint embeddings.

Through rigorous experimentation, I optimized this hybrid approach and evaluated it on three key downstream tasks: 3D Object Classification, Few-Shot Object Classification, and 3D Object Part Segmentation. Using benchmark datasets such as ShapeNet, ModelNet40, ScanObjectNN, and ShapeNetPart, my modifications to the CrossPoint architecture and integration with VICReg demonstrated notable performance improvements. These enhancements included better generalization to unseen data and superior robustness to domain shifts, marking a significant step forward in point cloud analysis. For more detailed results and analysis, please refer to my full thesis linked below.

This research exemplifies how augmenting cutting-edge architectures like CrossPoint with advanced regularization frameworks such as VICReg can enable scalable and efficient AI systems. These findings hold promising implications for real-world applications, from autonomous navigation to 3D content generation, where labeled data scarcity and domain adaptation are persistent hurdles.
